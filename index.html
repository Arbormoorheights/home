Frequently Asked

Quick answers.

When is rent due, and is there a grace period?+
Rent is due on the 1st of the month. A standard five-day grace period applies; late fees begin on the 6th unless otherwise stated in your lease. Paying via Zelle ensures your payment posts the same day.
What if I can't pay rent on time this month?+
Contact your property manager before the 1st. We work with residents in good standing on short-term arrangements far more often than people expect. Communication earns flexibility; silence does not.
How quickly will maintenance respond to my request?+
Emergencies — water, gas, heat in winter, no-AC in extreme heat, electrical hazards, or anything affecting safety — are responded to within four hours. Routine requests are typically scheduled within 48 hours.
Can I pay rent through other methods besides Zelle?+
Yes — we accept ACH transfer, cashier's check, and money order. However, Zelle is our preferred method because it's instant, fee-free, secure, and creates a clear record on both sides. Personal checks are accepted but slow.
When will I get my security deposit back after move-out?+
Florida law requires us to return your deposit, or send an itemized notice of any claim against it, within 15 days of move-out (or 30 days if a claim is made). We aim to be well inside those windows.
How do I start the lease renewal process?+
We reach out approximately 60 days before your lease ends, but you're welcome to start the conversation any time. Residents in good standing typically receive favorable renewal terms.
